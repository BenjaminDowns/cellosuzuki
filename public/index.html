Performing Experience

Rebeccah has performed throughout the United States, Canada, and Western Europe. She has been a featured chamber music performer at the Brevard Music Festival, the Bowdoin Music Festival, and many other chamber music series. As a soloist, she has performed the Elgar Cello Concerto, Lalo Cello Concerto and Faure Elegie. During her orchestral career she has served as principal or section cello with several orchestras including:

  • Springfield Symphony (principal)
  • University of Cincinnati Philharmonia (principal)
  • Kentucky Symphony
  • Lebanon Symphony

Teaching Experience

Rebeccah has taught cello for over 15 years privately, at community music schools, preparatory departments, and universities. A passionate educator, she specializes in Suzuki instruction and extremely young students, often beginning students at 2 years old. She currently maintains teaching studios at the MacPhail Center for Music in Minneapolis and her own private studio in Highland Park. She has previously served on the faculty of:

  • Brooklyn College Preparatory Program (New York City, NY)
  • Stony Brook University Community Music Program (Long Island, NY)
  • Port Jefferson Music Academy (Long Island, NY)
  • Wyoming Fine Arts Center (Cincinnati, OH)

Education History

Rebeccah's primary mentor was Yehuda Hanani, with whom she studied at the University of Cincinnati. She has also studied with Andres Diaz, Ari Lipsky, and Sharon Gerber.

Degrees and Certifications:

  • The School for Strings, Suzuki Certification with Pamela Devenport (2013)
  • University of Cincinnati (CCM), Artist Diploma in Cello Performance (2010)
  • University of Cincinnati (CCM), Master's of Music in Cello Performance (2006)
